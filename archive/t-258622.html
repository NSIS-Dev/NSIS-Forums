For uninstallers, it could mean there is a problem with your temporary directory. Make sure that you have write access to it and that it's not completely full.

For installers, it means that the installer can't open itself to read the data it needs. That could mean you have a problem with your hard disk, but I think it'd be a better guess to blame a new anti-virus.
